Stress is something everyone experiences at times, especially during pressure, uncertainty, change, or difficult life events.

A small amount of stress can sometimes help with focus or motivation, but too much stress can affect mental wellbeing and make it harder to cope day to day.

Common signs of stress may include feeling overwhelmed, irritable, tense, worried, unable to switch off, or mentally drained. Some people may also notice physical strain, tiredness, or trouble sleeping.

Supportive self-help approaches for stress may include:
- noticing what situations or patterns make stress worse
- taking short pauses during the day to reset attention
- using slow breathing or calming exercises
- making practical plans for manageable next steps
- building routines that support rest, movement, and recovery

Stress support content should stay practical, calm, and non-judgmental. It should not diagnose a condition or replace professional advice.

If stress is becoming difficult to manage or is affecting daily life significantly, it may help to seek support from a qualified professional or trusted health service.
