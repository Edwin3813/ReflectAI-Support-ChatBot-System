Panic attacks can feel frightening and intense. People may experience a sudden rush of fear, distress, or physical discomfort.

Support information about panic attacks should focus on calm coping steps and reassurance rather than diagnosis. Helpful ideas can include:
- moving attention to slow, steady breathing
- staying where possible until the intense feeling begins to pass
- reminding yourself that panic can feel overwhelming but will pass
- resting quietly afterwards if needed
- eating or drinking something if that helps you recover
- telling someone you trust if you feel able to

If panic attacks happen often, it may help to keep coping steps written down somewhere easy to find. It can also help to think about what kind of self-care is useful after an episode.

People close to someone experiencing panic may be able to help by staying calm, speaking gently, and giving reassurance without adding pressure.

This content is for supportive information only. It should not be used to diagnose a mental health condition or replace professional care.
