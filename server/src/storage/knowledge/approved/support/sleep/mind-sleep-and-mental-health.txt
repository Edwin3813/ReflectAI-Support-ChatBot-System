Sleep and mental health are closely linked. Poor sleep can make mental health feel worse, and mental health difficulties can also make it harder to sleep well.

People experiencing sleep problems may notice low energy, reduced concentration, irritability, or increased emotional strain. Worrying about sleep can also become part of the problem.

Supportive sleep guidance may include:
- building a steadier routine around going to bed and waking up
- trying not to put too much pressure on yourself to sleep immediately
- finding relaxing ways to wind down before bed
- making the sleeping environment more comfortable where possible
- keeping note of patterns if sleep difficulties continue
- looking for support with connected issues such as stress or anxiety

Different sleep strategies work for different people, and what helps can change over time. Sleep support content should remain practical and non-judgmental.

If sleep problems are persistent, severe, or affecting safety and daily life, it may help to seek professional support.
